# Discovery Call Guide: Supplier Payment Review

Date: 10 June 2026
Purpose: Keep a first conversation calm, useful, and commercially focused without drifting into technical demo mode.

## Aim Of The First Call

The first call is not to sell the portal.

The aim is to understand whether the organisation has enough supplier payment complexity, process friction, or working capital opportunity to justify a next step.

## Opening

Use this tone:

> Thanks for taking the time. I do not want to assume there is a project here. I would like to understand how supplier payment visibility and payment-related queries work today, then we can decide whether there is any useful next step or whether it is not relevant.

## 20-Minute Structure

| Time | Focus | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 mins | Context | Explain Corporate LinX briefly and agree the conversation scope |
| 2-8 mins | Current process | Understand invoice status, supplier queries, AP workflow, and ownership |
| 8-13 mins | Pain / value | Identify friction, cost, control gaps, or working capital interest |
| 13-17 mins | Fit | Decide whether review, early discounting feasibility, portal discussion, or no-fit makes sense |
| 17-20 mins | Next step | Agree one practical next action |

## Questions To Ask

### Supplier Visibility

1. Where do supplier invoice or payment queries usually land?
2. How do suppliers currently understand invoice status, approval progress, and expected payment timing?
3. Are supplier queries mostly routine status checks, exceptions, disputes, or payment timing issues?

### Internal Process

4. Which teams are involved between invoice receipt, approval, and payment?
5. Are there any manual spreadsheets, inboxes, portals, or workarounds in the process?
6. Where does avoidable effort appear most often?

### Finance And Working Capital

7. Is working capital improvement currently an active finance topic?
8. Have you used, reviewed, or rejected early payment discounting or supply chain finance before?
9. If cash-funded early payment created supplier benefit and commercial return, who would evaluate that?

### Fit And Buying Route

10. If this were worth reviewing, who would need to be involved?
11. What would make a first review easy to approve?
12. What would make this not worth pursuing?

## Listening For Buying Signals

- "Suppliers chase us constantly."
- "AP spend too much time answering status questions."
- "We do not have one clear view."
- "We have looked at supply chain finance before."
- "Treasury / CFO is focused on working capital."
- "Procurement owns supplier relationships but finance owns payment."
- "We are changing systems or reviewing processes."

## Listening For No-Fit Signals

- Very low supplier count
- No payment friction
- No finance or procurement ownership
- No appetite to share basic process information
- Need is purely an ERP implementation issue
- Stakeholder cannot identify any pain or owner

## Close Options

### If There Is Clear Interest

> It sounds like there may be enough here to justify a short review. I suggest we map the current supplier payment flow, identify where friction or value might sit, and then decide whether there is a business case for anything further.

### If The Person Is Not The Owner

> That makes sense. Who would be closest to supplier payment process, finance operations, AP, treasury, or procurement operations?

### If It Is Not Relevant

> That is useful to know. I would rather rule it out properly than force a conversation. If it becomes relevant later, the areas we usually help with are supplier visibility, payment process friction, and early discounting feasibility.
