# Corporate LinX UK Growth Plan

Date: 10 June 2026
Owner: Paul Ward
Objective: Start a repeatable UK new-client acquisition motion and begin outreach to at least one qualified account today.

## 1. What We Are Selling

Corporate LinX should not lead with "software" alone. The stronger commercial offer is:

1. Supplier payment visibility and control
2. Early discounting and supply chain finance programme enablement
3. Financial review, payment process review, and third-party audit to identify savings and operational improvement

The live website, documentation portal, and demo together support this positioning:

- Public site: Supply chain finance, dynamic discounting, P2P management, feasibility, finance, implementation
- Documentation: Supplier visibility portals, reverse factoring, discounting and payment management automation, workflow, B2B relationship management
- Demo: Admin, transactions, early payments, payments, summaries, charts, offer acceptance workflows

## 2. Best-Fit UK Client Profile

Priority profile:

- UK mid-market to enterprise buyers
- 100+ active suppliers, ideally far more
- Meaningful accounts payable volume
- Complex invoice/payment communication with suppliers
- Treasury, finance operations, or procurement leaders under pressure to improve working capital and control

Best-fit sectors:

- Manufacturing
- Food production and distribution
- Multi-site services businesses
- Transport, logistics, and infrastructure-heavy groups
- Housing and public-interest operators with large supplier estates

Preferred client type:

- Cash-generative businesses that could fund early payment offers themselves
- Businesses with enough supplier complexity that visibility, workflow, and reporting are painful
- Businesses already running, reviewing, or replacing a supply chain finance or supplier payment process

## 3. Core Offers To Lead With

### Offer A: Supplier Payment and Working Capital Review

Use this as the easiest first conversation.

What it gives the client:

- Current-state review of supplier payment visibility and control
- Review of AP, supplier comms, and payment timing friction
- Identification of discounting, funding, or process-improvement opportunities
- Clear commercial next-step recommendation

Why this matters:

- Easier to buy than a platform conversation
- Opens doors with CFO, treasury, AP, procurement, and COO stakeholders
- Lets Corporate LinX sell expertise before platform change

### Offer B: Early Discounting Feasibility Study

Lead with this for cash-strong businesses.

Position it as:

- A short review to determine whether the client can improve margin and supplier outcomes through self-funded early payment offers
- A fast "go / no-go" exercise rather than a big transformation project

### Offer C: Supplier Visibility Portal / SCF Platform

Lead with this when the pain is already obvious:

- Suppliers chasing invoice and payment status
- Poor reporting across entities
- Existing SCF process is operationally heavy or opaque
- Need for buyer, supplier, and funder coordination

## 4. Messaging That Should Win More Meetings

Do not lead with:

- "We build cloud infrastructure"
- Technical detail
- Generic "we save millions"

Lead with:

- Reduce supplier payment noise and escalation
- Improve visibility over invoice and payment status
- Identify margin gains through early discounting or better programme structure
- Give finance teams auditable control without ripping out ERP

Short positioning statement:

> Corporate LinX helps larger buyers reduce supplier payment friction, improve working capital control, and uncover discounting or process savings through a combination of specialist review work and a proven supplier payment platform.

2. The main CTA is generic and should become a clear diagnostic offer.
3. The site should explicitly mention financial review / third-party audit services.
4. The homepage should speak more directly to CFO, Treasury, AP, and Procurement outcomes.
5. One UK-facing case study or proof page would materially help conversion.

Recommended website additions:
