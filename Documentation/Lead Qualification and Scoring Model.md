# Lead Qualification and Scoring Model

Date: 10 June 2026
Purpose: Decide which prospects deserve time, research, and follow-up effort.

## Quick Rule

Prioritise companies where supplier payment complexity is likely, where finance or procurement benefit is easy to explain, and where a first relationship route is plausible.

Do not chase every recognisable logo. A smaller company with the right pain and a reachable stakeholder can be more valuable than a perfect enterprise target with no route in.

## Scorecard

Score each prospect from 0 to 3 in each category.

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Supplier complexity | Unknown or low | Some suppliers | Many suppliers | Large, complex, multi-entity supplier base |
| Payment friction likelihood | No visible reason | Possible | Likely due to sector or structure | Obvious from growth, complexity, supplier model, or payment-practice signal |
| Working capital fit | Weak | Some relevance | Clear finance relevance | Strong early discounting / cash-funded opportunity |
| Stakeholder reachability | No route | Generic contact only | Named department or likely route | Named person, warm route, or local relationship angle |
| Strategic fit | Outside core sectors | Adjacent fit | Good sector fit | Ideal sector and scale |
| Timing trigger | None | General business activity | Growth, change, procurement activity, or investment | Clear event creating urgency |

## Priority Bands

- 14 to 18: Priority A. Research and personalise before contact.
- 10 to 13: Priority B. Worth contacting with a lighter touch.
- 6 to 9: Priority C. Hold for later or use only if a route appears.
- 0 to 5: Do not pursue unless new information changes the picture.

## Ideal Client Signals

- Multi-site operations
- Manufacturing, food, logistics, infrastructure, housing, or services with large supplier estates
- Growth investment, acquisition, restructuring, ERP activity, or finance transformation
- Public procurement activity or supplier guidance pages
- Supplier payment complaints, poor payment-practice indicators, or visible AP friction
- Cash generative business where early payment discounting could be self-funded

## Disqualification Signals

- Too small to have meaningful supplier payment complexity
- No plausible buyer or route into the organisation
- Already has a highly mature supplier finance function with no visible trigger
- Sector has weak fit or long procurement route without a strong reason to engage
- Only product interest is "software demo" without finance or operational owner involvement

## Research Checklist Before First Contact

1. Company size, locations, and operating model
2. Supplier footprint or procurement pages
3. Named stakeholders in procurement, finance operations, treasury, AP, or CFO office
4. Local, sector, growth, or timing reason for contact
5. Best opening angle: local relationship, supplier visibility, working capital, early discounting, or process review
6. One sentence explaining why this company, not any company

## Example Scoring Notes

Use short notes, not essays:

- "Andover HQ; manufacturing/distribution; public purchasing route; growth investment visible."
- "Large supplier spend; procurement leadership visible; review/audit angle stronger than early discounting."
- "Strong enterprise fit but no named route yet; hold until stakeholder found."
