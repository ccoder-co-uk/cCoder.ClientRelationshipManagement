# Target Account Shortlist

Date: 10 June 2026
Purpose: A practical shortlist for first UK outreach based on fit, local relevance, and visible supplier/procurement complexity.

## 1. Stannah

Location:

- Andover, Hampshire

Why it is a strong fit:

- Headquarters, manufacturing, and worldwide distribution operations are based in Andover
- Global subsidiaries and a visible purchasing function suggest meaningful supplier and payment complexity
- Growth investment is visible through the new Andover factory announcement

Commercial angle:

- Supplier payment visibility across a growing manufacturing and distribution environment
- Review whether early payment, supplier portal visibility, or process improvements could reduce friction and support growth

Likely first functions to target:

- Purchasing / Procurement
- Finance Operations
- Accounts Payable transformation

Recommended first move:

- Call the Andover office and ask who owns supplier payment process improvement, procurement operations, or AP efficiency

Public route already visible:

- Stannah's purchasing site publicly lists Purchasing Manager Tiffany Howell with Andover purchasing contact details

## 2. Vitacress

Location:

- Hampshire / Andover area

Why it is a strong fit:

- Multi-country growing and supply network
- Supplies major retailers across Europe
- Explicitly references a network of suppliers and multi-retailer supply

Commercial angle:

- Improve visibility and control across supplier and payment workflows
- Explore whether group entities or supplier categories are suitable for early discounting or process improvement

Likely first functions to target:

- Finance
- Procurement
- Commercial operations

Recommended first move:

- Target finance or procurement leadership with a review-led approach rather than a platform-first approach

Public route already visible:

- Vitacress lists its St Mary Bourne, Andover site and a general contact route on its contact page

## 3. Aster Group

Location:

- South of England and London

Why it is a useful target:

- Spends around GBP 110 million annually with suppliers
- Owns and manages over 37,000 homes
- Has a visible procurement leadership function and large investment programme

Commercial angle:

- Financial review and third-party audit to identify savings, supplier process friction, and payment workflow improvements
- Better fit for review / audit / process improvement than for early discounting as a first discussion

Likely first functions to target:

- Director of Procurement
- Finance transformation
- Supplier management

Recommended first move:

- Use a procurement and operational-efficiency angle, not a pure SCF pitch

## 4. Serco

Location:

- Hook, Hampshire

Why it is worth targeting:

- Large UK and international operating footprint
- High contract and supplier complexity
- Large enough to care about payment control, governance, and working capital discipline

Commercial angle:

- Specialist review of supplier payment visibility, AP workflow, and discounting feasibility inside business units where supplier complexity is highest

Likely first functions to target:

- Procurement excellence
- Finance operations
- Shared services leadership

Recommended first move:

- Approach as a targeted review or pilot conversation, not a company-wide transformation

## 5. Carnival UK

Location:

- Southampton, Hampshire

Why it is worth targeting:

- Southampton-based operation
- Part of a very large group with an extensive supplier ecosystem
- Public supplier and business partner framework suggests meaningful supplier governance and process complexity

Commercial angle:

- Supplier payment visibility and workflow review for multi-brand, multi-supplier operations
- Early discounting may be relevant if cash position and category mix support it

Likely first functions to target:

- Finance transformation
- Procurement
- Shared services

Recommended first move:

- Target the review / process-improvement offer first, then qualify discounting fit

## Why Stannah Is The Best First Account Today

- It is local to you in Andover
- It is large enough to have real supplier and payment complexity
- The growth story gives you a timely business reason to start a conversation
- The commercial fit is clearer than a broad public-sector style prospect

## Suggested Qualification Questions For Every Target

1. Do they have enough supplier volume to make payment visibility painful?
2. Is there a procurement or finance change agenda already underway?
3. Would they respond better to a review / audit offer or a platform conversation?
4. Is there likely to be a cash-funded early discounting angle?
5. Can Paul credibly reach a decision-maker or a warm internal route within 5 business days?

## Notes

- This shortlist is intentionally practical rather than exhaustive.
- The goal for today is one live outreach, not perfect market coverage.
