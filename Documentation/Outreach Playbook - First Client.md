# Outreach Playbook

Date: 10 June 2026
Use case: First-contact outreach for UK target accounts.

Related guidance: See `Email Authoring Guidance Notes.md` for email opening, salutation, and tone rules.

## 1. How To Position Yourself

Do not sound like a technical implementation lead.

Use this framing:

> Corporate LinX works with larger businesses to improve supplier payment visibility, identify working capital and discounting opportunities, and reduce supplier friction without forcing ERP replacement.

## 2. Best First Offer

Lead with a short review, not a demo.

Recommended offer:

> A short supplier payment and working capital review to identify where payment visibility, supplier friction, or early discounting could create measurable value.

Why this works:

- Easier to say yes to
- Business-first
- Opens doors with procurement and finance people
- Lets the client decide whether a bigger conversation is worthwhile

## 3. Call Script For Reception / Gatekeeper

Use this almost word for word:

> Hello, this is Paul Ward from Corporate LinX. We help larger businesses improve supplier payment visibility and identify working capital and process savings. I am trying to find the best person responsible for procurement operations, finance operations, or accounts payable improvement. Who would be the right person to speak with?

If challenged:

> This is not a generic software sales call. We usually begin with a short review to identify whether there is a fit, then either progress or step away quickly.

## 9. Discovery Questions For The First Call

1. Where do supplier payment queries and escalation currently land?
2. How visible is invoice and payment status across teams and suppliers today?
3. Are there parts of AP or supplier communications that feel manual or noisy?
4. Have you explored early discounting, supply chain finance, or supplier funding before?
5. Is there pressure internally to improve working capital, control, or supplier experience?
6. If value existed, what would make a first review easy to approve?

## 10. What Success Looks Like Today

On 10 June 2026, success is not closing a deal.

Success is:

1. One phone conversation or referral
2. One tailored email sent
3. One LinkedIn connection sent
4. One clear next step for follow-up
