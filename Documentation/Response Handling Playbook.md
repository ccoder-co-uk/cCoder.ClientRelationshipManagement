# Response Handling Playbook

Date: 10 June 2026
Purpose: Respond consistently when a prospect replies, redirects, objects, or ignores the first contact.

## Principle

Every reply is useful. Even "not me" can become a route to the right person.

Keep replies short, human, and specific. Do not over-explain Corporate LinX unless the prospect asks for detail.

## If They Say "Send More Information"

Subject:

Re: Local introduction

Body:

Hi [Name],

Thanks, appreciated.

I have attached a short one-page overview. It is not a product brochure; it simply sets out what we usually look at in a first supplier payment / working capital review.

If it looks even loosely relevant, I would be happy to have a brief introductory call. If not, no problem at all.

Best regards,
Paul

Attachment:

Supplier Payment Review - One Page Overview.pdf

Hi [Name],

That can mean reviewing where supplier queries, invoice status visibility, AP workflow friction, or early payment discounting opportunities sit. If there is a fit, we can support that with review work and portal infrastructure. If there is not, we rule it out quickly.

The first useful step is usually a short conversation to understand whether the problem exists.

Paul

## If They Do Not Reply

Follow-up after 3 to 5 business days:

Hi [Name],

Just following up on my note below.

No pressure at all if this is not relevant at the moment. I am mainly trying to introduce Corporate LinX properly and understand whether supplier payment visibility or working capital improvement is ever something [Company] reviews.

If there is someone better placed than you, I would appreciate being pointed in the right direction.

Best regards,
Paul

## When To Stop

Stop after:

- One initial email
- One gentle follow-up
- One final referral-style check if there is a strong reason

Do not keep chasing a cold contact unless there is a specific business trigger or a new route into the account.
