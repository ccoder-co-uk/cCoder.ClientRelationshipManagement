# Weekly Lead Generation Operating Rhythm

Date: 10 June 2026
Purpose: Keep growth activity consistent without turning it into a noisy campaign.

## Weekly Target

Keep the volume small and the quality high.

- 3 researched prospects per week
- 2 first-contact emails per week
- 1 follow-up block per week
- 1 improvement to messaging or materials per week

## Monday: Pipeline Review

1. Review active client threads
2. Check follow-up dates in the tracker
3. Choose this week's 2 target accounts
4. Decide the opening angle for each account

## Tuesday: Research And Personalisation

1. Find the likely buyer or referral route
2. Check company site, procurement pages, annual reports, news, and payment-practice signals where available
3. Score each prospect using the qualification model
4. Write one company-specific reason for contact

## Wednesday: Outreach

1. Send no more than 1 to 2 first-contact emails
2. Log each email in the tracker
3. Prepare the follow-up date immediately

## Thursday: Relationship Building

1. Send LinkedIn connection requests where appropriate
2. Look for local business groups, supplier events, finance/procurement networks, and warm paths
3. Identify one non-cold route into a future prospect

## Friday: Learning Loop

1. Record replies, silence, objections, and referrals
2. Improve one sentence, template, or asset based on what happened
3. Update the next week's target list

## Weekly Questions

- Which accounts are showing signs of life?
- Which outreach felt too sales-like?
- Which sector seems easiest to explain?
- Which contact routes are working?
- Do we have enough proof to support the claims we are making?

## Avoid

- Sending many generic emails
- Attaching documents to cold first emails by default
- Moving to demo language too quickly
- Chasing huge companies without a route in
- Treating silence as failure before one polite follow-up
