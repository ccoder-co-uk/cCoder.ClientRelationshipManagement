# Target Account Shortlist - Higher Budget South and London Wave 1

Date: 15 June 2026
Purpose: Fast review list for the next outreach block, prioritising bigger budgets, more supplier complexity, and stronger finance / procurement routes.

## Recommended Send Order

### 1. Mitie Group

Why first:

- Very strong fit to supplier payment visibility and AP friction
- Direct public CFO email found
- Large supplier and subcontractor base
- London-based commercial context
- Easier to position as practical process improvement rather than a large transformation

Recommended owner route:

- Simon Kirkpatrick, Chief Financial Officer

Why now:

- Strongest mix of scale, contactability, and likely relevance

### 2. Southern Water

Why second:

- Direct public CFO email and phone number
- Large contractor and supplier base
- Major investment cycle creating timing relevance
- South of England regional relevance

Recommended owner route:

- Stuart Ledger, Chief Financial Officer

Why now:

- Very actionable contact data and a credible finance-control angle

### 3. Balfour Beatty

Why third:

- Very large budget and supplier complexity
- Explicit procurement strategy and supply-chain focus in public materials
- London flagship account
- Good CPO route and finance expansion route

Recommended owner route:

- Evan Sutherland, Chief Procurement Officer

Why now:

- Strong strategic fit, but message needs to be tighter and more procurement-aware than Mitie or Southern Water

### 4. Wates Group

Why fourth:

- Strong South East fit
- Record forward order book
- Executive-level supply-chain ownership now visible
- More reachable-feeling than some public mega-accounts

Recommended owner route:

- Philip Wainwright, Chief Financial Officer

Why now:

- Good balance of local relevance and commercial scale

### 5. Thames Water

Why fifth:

- Huge budget and obvious supplier complexity
- Senior routes identified
- Strong long-term value if opened

Recommended owner route:

- Steve Buck, Chief Financial Officer

Why fifth:

- High value, but also highest risk of low responsiveness due to current business pressure and public scrutiny

## Reserve Account

### National Highways

Reason to keep in reserve:

- Very large procurement footprint
- Strong official procurement contact evidence
- Good direct-email environment
- Likely slower route than the private-sector accounts above
